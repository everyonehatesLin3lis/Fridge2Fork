# Cooking Feasibility Rules

Use these checks when auditing a recipe:

1. Are all ingredients used in physically realistic ways?
2. Are liquid, creamy, or soft ingredients not described as cuttable pieces?
3. Does the dish name match the ingredients and method?
4. If the dish says pie, skillet, bake, casserole, omelette, or wrap, is there a binder or base?
5. Will high heat cause curdling, burning, watery texture, or splitting?
6. Are missing ingredients essential or optional?
7. Does every used ingredient have a measurable per-portion and total amount?
8. Do portions, cooking time, and method scale realistically?
9. Are allergy conflicts removed instead of casually substituted?
10. If invalid, rewrite toward a simpler valid dish form.

Common repairs:

- Mostly soft dairy + deli meat: cold protein bowl, spread plate, or wrap filling.
- Eggs + vegetables: omelette, scramble, or egg skillet.
- Grain/starch base + toppings: warm bowl.
- Tortilla/lavash/bread + fillings: wrap.
- No binder/base: avoid pie, bake, casserole, and crispy skillet names.
